The Computational Friction Loom

1. The Silent Sniper

Blockchain networks breathe. During peak hours, gas fees soar. But when the network sleeps and fees crash to the Dead Zone (1.05 Gwei on BSC), our autonomous node wakes up. It acts as a silent guardian, monitoring every block created every 3 seconds.

2. Proof of Burn

When the dead zone is detected, the protocol triggers a smart contract that executes massive, arbitrary computational loops. This intentional friction is designed to do one thing: burn cheap gas. The network's idle energy is consumed.

3. Digital Alchemy

In exchange for the mathematically proven destruction of gas, the smart contract mints $ASH directly to the wallet. $ASH is not created from nothing; it is the physical representation of burned computational effort.

Self-Sustaining Tokenomics

Auto-Bootstrapping LP

The contract is fully autonomous. Upon receiving initial liquidity, it directly calls the PancakeSwap Factory. It creates the trading pair (WBNB/ASH) and adds liquidity automatically, shifting from a script to a living economic system.

The Hedging Mechanism

$ASH acts as a natural index for BSC Gas prices. When the network is cheap, $ASH supply inflates steadily. When the network is congested and expensive, the Loom stops. Minting halts entirely, making existing $ASH highly scarce.

Intrinsic Value

Unlike meme tokens, $ASH has a verifiable production cost. Investors and bots acquire $ASH because its scarcity is backed by hard mathematics and the physical limits of the Binance Smart Chain's transaction throughput.
